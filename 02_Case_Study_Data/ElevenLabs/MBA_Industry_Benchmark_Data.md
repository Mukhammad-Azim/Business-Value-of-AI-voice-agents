# MBA Industry Benchmark Data — Cost to Originate
## Source: Mortgage Bankers Association (MBA), 2024 Annual Mortgage Bankers Performance Report
## URL: https://www.mba.org

---

## Key Benchmark Figures

| Year | Avg. Total Loan Production Expense Per Loan | Source |
|------|------------------------------------------------|--------|
| 2023 | **$11,258 per loan** | MBA Annual Report |
| 2024 | **$11,076 per loan** | MBA Annual Report |

## Details
- Includes: commissions, compensation, occupancy, equipment, other production expenses, and corporate allocations
- Sample: Independent mortgage banks and mortgage subsidiaries of chartered banks
- In 2024, lenders reported average net production profit of **$443 per loan** — marking a return to aggregate profitability after two consecutive years of losses (2022–2023)
- Costs/profitability vary significantly based on production volume, product mix, geography, and operational efficiency
- Larger lenders benefit from economies of scale

## Labor Cost Component (from Better.com Q2 2025)
- Industry average labor cost to fund: **$6,500–$7,000 per loan** (per Kevin Ryan, CFO, Better.com)
- Better.com D2C labor cost to fund in Q2 2025: **under $2,500 per loan**

## Usage in Thesis
- These benchmarks form the denominator for the "cost advantage %" calculations in Table 5.3
- The 2023 figure ($11,258) is used for Q3–Q4 2023 comparisons
- The 2024 figure ($11,076) is used for Q1 2024–Q2 2025 comparisons
